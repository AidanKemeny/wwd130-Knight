Long Legs

(Rated R) (Scary Level: 8)

Long legs was a disturbing movie on my list. It begins by showing a little girl outside of a house in the winter and a car driving up to the house with what appears to be no one in the driver’s seat. Then there’s a man right in front of the girl. This starts off the movie with the audience asking a lot of questions. Throughout the movie you will find a lot of suspense with a moderate level of gore. There are also themes of dolls and satanism. This movie is not one you would want to watch at night.

A Quiet Place:

(Rated PG13) (Scary Level: 3)

A Quiet Place is a more milder horror movie on my list. There isn’t too much gore or swearing. I t is a good movie to start with if you are very sensitive to horror.

The Village

(Rated PG13) (Scary Level: 2)

The village is a milder movie for those who may be more sensitive, but still a very interesting story with a big twist at the end. It takes place in a small village. The people there can’t leave because the surrounding woods around them has a beast that will come for them if they cross a certain line or are wearing a certain color. This is great movie for those who love M. Night Shyamalan movies such as The Sixth Sense, Trap, Old, or unexpected twists.

A Nightmre on Elm Street

(Rated R) (Scary Level: 7)

This is one of the classic slasher horror movies most are familiar with.

The Descent

(Rated R) (Scary Level: 9)

I was genuinely scared watching this movie. You will be screaming at the characters for their decisions almost all the way through, and the ending might make you mad. A group of girls decide to venture down into a cave and are met with some interesting creatures, to say the least. The feelings invoked felt like I was watching the movie Fall again. This movie is worth the watch if you want to be scared at night and never go into a cave again.
